UX Strategy · June 2026

From landing page to website

Today the whole of boutiquecapitalpartners.co.uk is one long scroll. This document maps a proper multi-page architecture: who the site serves, how the best firms in the industry structure theirs, the proposed sitemap, and what the new slimmer homepage does instead.

01 · Diagnosis

Why the one-pager is holding BCP back

Today
  • Two very different audiences share one scroll. A fund manager looking to outsource distribution and a wealth-manager fund buyer hunting for the River Road OEIC wade through the same ~10 sections.
  • Nothing is addressable. Services, team bios and the fund live at #anchors — they can't be linked in an email, indexed by Google, or sent to a compliance officer.
  • The deepest content is the most buried. The 8 services and the fund proposition — BCP's commercial core — sit behind clicks inside widgets at the bottom of a long page.
  • One page = one story order. Everyone gets hero → intro → River Road → services → team… regardless of what they came for.
Proposed
  • Self-selection in the hero. Two doors — "I manage funds" / "I buy funds" — each leading to a purpose-built journey (the pattern used by the strongest firms in the sector).
  • Every key asset gets a URL. Each service, the fund, every insight, every bio — shareable, indexable, citable.
  • The homepage becomes a router. A confident first impression plus clear paths — not the whole firm in one scroll.
  • Compliance by design. The professional-investors disclaimer moves from footer small-print to a remembered, one-time attestation where regulations expect it (fund pages).
02 · Competitive scan

What the best in the industry do

Patterns from the strongest asset-management and boutique-manager sites (GQG Partners, Acadian, Dimensional, Two Sigma, Sculptor, Axonic, Brevan Howard) — and what BCP should borrow or beat.

GQG · Invesco

Audience self-selection up front — visitors choose investor type / region and the site adapts. Boutique scale, big-firm clarity.

→ BCP's version: a two-door hero (fund managers vs fund buyers). No popup gate; just honest wayfinding.

Acadian · Dimensional

The hero works hard: direct links to strategies and philosophy, animated credibility stats, and news/insights surfaced beside the headline rather than wasted white space.

→ Keep BCP's floating "Viewpoint" callout — it's already this pattern. Add proof numbers (jurisdictions, AUM raised, years of distribution experience).

Two Sigma · Axonic · Sculptor

Content is the moat: research made freely available, a real insights hub with categories, newsletter capture, and a firm story told properly (timeline, video, values).

→ BCP already writes genuine commentary. Give it a hub with filters, an article template, and email capture — that's how a 2-person brand outpunches its size.

03 · Audiences & journeys

Two audiences, two journeys

Everything on the site serves one of these two visitors (plus press/partners as a minor third). Each journey should reach its goal in two clicks from the homepage.

Boutique fund managers

FM · supply side

"Can BCP get my strategy in front of UK & European intermediaries — and are they credible?"

Home→ For Fund Managers→ Service detail→ Partner with us

Proof they need on the way: the 8-service offering, Rodger's track record & the Advisory Board, the River Road case study, how a partnership works step-by-step.

Fund buyers / intermediaries

FB · demand side

"What strategies does BCP distribute, how do I access them, and where are the documents?"

Home→ Fund Partners→ Fund page→ Document centre / Enquire

Proof they need: manager pedigree, vehicle & platform availability, fee waivers, KIIDs/factsheets, and ongoing commentary that shows conviction.

05 · Evaluation — how loud should the fund be?

Selectivity is the brand. Don't merchandise it.

BCP's whole positioning is the filter: hundreds of conversations, almost all of them "no". A funds-forward homepage — the right pattern for Invesco or Calamos with hundreds of products — sends the opposite signal when the roster is one. Three options, evaluated:

Option A · Fund-forward

The asset-manager default: "Funds" first in nav, fund cards in the hero, performance front and centre. Built for firms whose product breadth is the pitch.

→ Rejected. With one partner it reads thin — a supermarket with one shelf — and repositions BCP as River Road's marketing arm rather than the selector.

Option B · Two-door router

Equal hero cards: "I manage funds" / "I buy funds". Honest about the two audiences and gets everyone where they're going fast.

→ Demoted. Efficient but characterless — the homepage becomes an airport. Both visitors get a corridor before anyone hears why BCP is different. Keep the split, but at the contact layer, not the hero.

Option C · Selectivity-led ✓

The homepage tells the curation story; River Road appears once, generously — framed as what passing the filter looks like, not as inventory. "Hundreds considered. One chosen."

→ Recommended. Scarcity raises perceived quality for buyers and flatters prospective managers (a select list, not a stable). And it scales — at 3–4 partners the same module becomes a small curated set, never a grid.

Guardrails so buyers don't pay for the romance

"Fund Partners" stays in the nav with a dropdown straight to the fund page and document centre (≤2 clicks from anywhere); every insight article cross-links the fund it discusses; and the buyer's contact path ("arrange a meeting") exists from the first screen — just quieter than the manager CTA, because the site's relationship-led sales motion means buyers usually arrive already knowing why.

06 · The new homepage

From 10 sections to 5

The homepage stops being the whole site and starts being its best salesperson: identity, segmentation, proof, freshness, action — in one viewport-and-a-half.

0 · NAV
Sticky header
Fund Managers · Fund Partners · Insights · About · Contact + "Partner with us" button
1 · HERO
Wave photo + strapline + one door
"Outsourced distribution for outstanding investment strategies." One coral CTA: Partner with us. Quiet buyer path beneath: "Access our fund partners →". Floating Viewpoint callout stays.
2 · PROOF
Selectivity strip
Stats that tell the filter story: strategies reviewed → partnerships signed · jurisdictions covered · years' distribution experience · advisory board depth
3 · FEATURE
Proof of selectivity
"Hundreds considered. One chosen." River Road presented generously → fund page. Scales to a curated few as the roster grows — never a grid.
4 · INSIGHTS
Latest 3 insights
InsightCards + "View all" → hub. Newsletter capture inline.
5 · CLOSE
Best-in-class CTA + footer
"Best-in-class fund manager? Let's have a conversation." → Contact. Compliance footer unchanged.

What moves off the homepage

The 8-service explorer → For Fund Managers. Team grid + bios → About. Values strip → About. Puffin/CSR → About / Responsibility. River Road deep-dive → Fund Partners. Nothing is deleted — everything gets a better home and a URL.

What's genuinely new

The selectivity narrative and strip, the "How we partner" process page, the article template, newsletter capture, intent-split contact, and a remembered professional-investor attestation on fund documents.

What we deliberately keep

The brand system as-is (navy/coral/cream, Montserrat, lifts and coral accents), the floating Viewpoint callout, the question-led section titles, and the warm "We…" voice. This is an IA change, not a rebrand.

Where we beat the field

Most boutique-distribution sites pick one audience and bore the other. BCP can be the rare site that genuinely serves both sides of its market — and the puffin partnership gives it a human warmth none of the references have.
