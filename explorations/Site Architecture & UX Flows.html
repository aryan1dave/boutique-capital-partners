UX Proposal · June 2026

From landing page to website:
a structure for boutiquecapitalpartners.co.uk

Today everything BCP says lives on one long homepage. The proposal: a five-section site with two clear audience journeys — fund managers who want distribution, and fund buyers who want funds — with the homepage rebuilt as a router, not a brochure.

What the best in the industry do

Four patterns to beat the field with

01 · Audience-first routing

Two front doors

Top firms segment by investor type immediately. BCP genuinely has two audiences — managers (supply) and intermediaries (demand) — but today both get the same scroll. Route them within the first screen.

02 · Funds ≤ 2 clicks away

Fund pages are the product

Fund buyers judge a distributor by how fast they reach fund facts, docs and commentary. River Road deserves a real fund page — performance, platforms, share classes, document centre — not a mid-page announcement.

03 · Insights as engine

A hub, not a teaser

Leading sites treat commentary as the repeat-visit engine: filterable hub, strong article template, related funds. BCP's three cards become a destination that feeds both journeys.

04 · Short homepage

Say less, link more

The best homepages in the sector are short, modular and confident — each section is a doorway to a deeper page. Depth moves into the site; the homepage earns the click.

UX audit

What changes, and why

Today — one page does everything

  • ~9 full sections stacked on one URL; no scent of a "site" beyond the dropdown.
  • 8 services live inside an interactive widget — invisible to scanning, search engines and deep links.
  • River Road, the proof point, is an announcement block — no data, no documents, no journey.
  • Team bios trapped in modals; values trapped in popups. Nothing is linkable.
  • Insights = 3 cards and a "view all" with nowhere real to go.
  • One CTA tone ("Partner with us") even though half the visitors are buyers, not managers.

Proposed — five sections, two journeys

  • Five top-level sections, every key fact gets a URL: services, fund, team member, article.
  • Services become a scannable overview page + anchor-linked detail — the widget becomes navigation.
  • A real Funds section: River Road partner page → fund page → document centre.
  • About becomes a home for the story: team grid with bio pages, values, puffin/CSR.
  • Insights hub with categories and an article template that cross-sells the fund.
  • Homepage routes by audience: "I manage a fund" vs "I buy funds" — twin CTAs from the hero down.

Everything keeps its brand skin

This is a re-plumbing, not a re-skin: cream/navy/teal section rhythm, Montserrat scale, coral CTAs, swirl watermark and wave hero all carry straight over from the design system.

The homepage gets ~60% shorter

Services widget, team grid, values popups and the full puffin story move to their own URLs. Each homepage section is a confident doorway with one link — scroll time drops, click-through and SEO depth rise.

Twin CTAs everywhere

The single "Partner with us" tone assumes every visitor is a manager. Buyers need their own verbs: "View the fund", "Download documents", "Arrange a meeting". Both paths should be present from hero to footer.

Compliance gets stronger, not louder

A one-time professional-investor gate (remembered) covers the FCA requirement explicitly, and lets individual pages breathe. Footer disclaimer stays verbatim on every page.

Build order

1) New homepage · 2) Fund page + document centre (highest buyer value) · 3) Services overview · 4) About with bio pages · 5) Insights hub. Each step ships independently.
